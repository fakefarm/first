Art & Design Glossary

Let's make sure we understand one another.

Primary Design – The main focal point of the tee design.

Secondary Design – A complimentary component of the design that adds depth and interest.

The 5 Design Elements

(Design Elements is a collective term that encompasses the following terms)

1. Type Treatment – How the text will be designed. Examples include “blocky”, “Serif”, “Vintage”, etc.

2. Icon – Specific subject matter.

3. Texture – A process of adding additional feel. Examples include “distressed”, “jersey”, “worn in”

4. Pattern – A repeating image, line, or shape.

5. Flourish – A flow of swirls, waves, or lines.

Additional Terminology

Colorway – Refers to the combination of art color(s) and product color.

Colors – Refers to the number of colors used in the design.

Sketch – A pre-design visual guide to the design project.

Layer – How the design elements lay on each other. Pertaining to icons, flourishes, type, and patterns.

Layout – The way all the design elements are put together and presented on the garment.

Revision – An edit to the design that involves text, textures, color ways, or design size.

Revisions do not create a new Primary Design.

Modifications - A complete make over of the design. To the point it could be a re-work from the beginning.
