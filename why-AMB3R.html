Nobody Needs Another T-shirt

Anyone can print you a t-shirt. But it takes skill to create a t-shirt people wear to places other than the gym. Customers buy because your shirt looks and feels good. Sure they may say they support your cause, but if your shirt does not evoke a self-interested emotional response – forget about it.

We focus on building demand for your brand… not supply. The hard part is making a tee that people actually want to buy and wear anywhere. That’s the real work we do; Helping you generate a higher profit and little remaining inventory after the event because people are actually asking for your tees!

Our mission is to create the coolest t-shirt in your customer’s closet within your deadline and budget.

Two reasons to try a Design Kit for your next t-shirt.

1. Clip art is easy to buy, but it's not easy to wear. The quality, uniqueness, and originality tends to underwhelm. Your cause is unique and a pre-made design does not describe your passion as best it could. The up front cost of Design Kits might be more than clip-art, but the long-term use will more than pay for itself.

2. Designing for the apparel medium is different than designing for print, or web. There are specifics and techniques that professional apparel designers know, that marketing 'total package' designer typically do not. AMB3R Design Kits are print-ready and designed with in your deadline and budget.

Our Fashion Designer applies current trends to your t-shirt

Have you ever heard the phrase "Just because you can, doesn't mean you should". We have 14 ways to a print a t-shirt, but be assured, we are not advocating gaudy, or tacky uses. Our suggestions are based on trends and styles found at your local mall where multi-million dollar brands have invested in research and development to identify styles that customers will wear. We study their findings and apply the insights to your t-shirt.

History

Jeremy and Dave met on tour in the Summer of 2001. Jeremy was selling music merchandise and Dave was selling his clothing line. They spent the Summer driving from music festival to music festival selling t-shirts out of the back of a truck for bands. They stayed in touch and in 2008 decided it was time to turn their knowledge of designing, printing, and selling t-shirts into a company.

Team

Jeremy Picker

Twitter | Email

Jeremy oversees AMB3R’s Design and Development team. He was introduced to the world of apparel at 12 years old, helping out his older brother at Truth Soul Armor. Jeremy studied fashion design and has worked on projects for Abercrombie & Fitch, Hurly, and Nike. He toured for 10 years selling music merchandise for major label bands. Jeremy's passion is helping non-profits raise funds and awareness. He started HEYtee.com to support Haiti after the 2010 earthquake. Dave Woodall
